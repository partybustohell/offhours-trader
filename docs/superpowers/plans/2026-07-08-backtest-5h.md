# Five-Hour Backtest Protocol — compressed from the rev-2 plan

**Question:** same as the full plan — does this system work at defaults — but the answer must land within 5 hours of wall clock (build + data + run + report), given API keys are already in `.env`.

**Design change, not a trim:** the full plan walks 124 trading days sequentially because equity compounds and positions carry over. In 5 hours that serialization is unaffordable — and it buys little: theses expire the next weekday at 20:00, so nearly all position life fits inside a two-day span. The compressed protocol therefore replays **independent episodes**: each sampled day D runs fresh with $50,000 — thesis at D 17:00, production `runTick` gating at 15-minute cadence through D 20:00 and D+1 04:00→20:00 (full Alpaca DAY order lifetime), force-flatten at D+1 20:00. Episodes share nothing, so LLM calls AND simulation parallelize fully, and per-episode P&L on fixed capital gives cleaner trade-level statistics than a compounded curve at this sample size.

**Episode boundary correction (production parity):** left alone, an episode would let day D's thesis take NEW entries during D+1 17:00–20:00 — a window production never grants it, because production's ~17:05 pipeline writes a D+1 thesis that `loadUnexpiredThesis(today)` picks up first. So at sim D+1 17:05 the episode writes a synthetic empty D+1 thesis (`entries: []`, real `thesisPath`/`thesisExpiry`) into its OUT_DIR: new entries from D's thesis stop exactly as in production, while the executor's expiry-ignoring yesterday-file lookup keeps D's open positions judge-monitored through the force-flatten. Pre-registered scope statement: an episode measures only D's thesis lifecycle; D+1's real thesis is out of scope. Integration test: an unfilled D entry whose band is crossed at D+1 18:30 places no order after the 17:15 tick, while an open position whose invalidation triggers at D+1 18:30 still produces a judge exit.

## Day sampling — 50 episodes from 124 trading days, two strata

- **Stratum R (random, n=30):** uniform sample of trading days. Sole source of the unbiased abstention-rate estimate (30 days → 95% CI half-width ≤ ~18pp; enough to separate "never trades" from "trades weekly").
- **Stratum H (high-dispersion, n=20):** after the universe daily-bar download, rank all days by cross-sectional dispersion — count of symbols with |day-over-day move| ≥ 5% and 20-day avg dollar volume ≥ $20M (SIP) — and take the top 20 not already in R. These are the days the discovery layer feeds on; they maximize the chance of observing actual entries, exits, judge vetoes, and risk-gate rejections with real teeth.
- Strata are never pooled for rate estimates. Abstention/behavior rates come from R alone; H exists to stress the mechanical and behavioral paths. H is an upper bound on **activity** (entries, vetoes, rejections, fills); the direction of its P&L bias is unknown and regime-dependent — never claimed as conservative or favorable.
- **Economic reporting rule:** the headline economic number is the bootstrap CI on per-episode net P&L from stratum R only (n=30, honestly wide). H-stratum economics appear in a separate subsection labeled "conditional on top-16% cross-sectional-dispersion days (deterministic selection)" — no pooled headline. If one full-window number is wanted, a design-weighted stratified estimate is computed (H stratum censused, weight 20/124; remainder estimated from the R draws outside it, weight 104/124) and labeled as such.
- **Pre-committed drop ordering:** at sample-selection time, a drop-priority ordering is persisted to disk alongside the sample (R in random-draw order, H in descending dispersion rank), and the Phase A scheduler dispatches episodes in that order. If the T+2:30 checkpoint fires, the kept set is the first 24 R + first 16 H of the pre-committed order — maximally complete by construction, no informative censoring. Degraded precision is pre-registered: R=24 gives 95% CI half-width ≤ ~20pp; the report states which rule applied.
- Post-cutoff rule from §0 of the rev-2 plan still applies: sampling is drawn from post-cutoff days only if any model cutoff intrudes on the window.

## What is kept from the rev-2 plan (all of it cheap, none of it optional)

- **SIP minute bars / quotes / trades** for fills (the IEX extended-hours hole is fatal at any budget), the T1 feed probe, `adjustment=raw`, split-day exclusion, borrow-flag hard gate, date-keyed 2026 fee schedule.
- **Real production functions everywhere:** `runTick` with injected ledger/marketData/llm, `buildCandidates`, `computeThesisEntries`, `writeNarratives` merge (narratives are a trading input), `seedDeployedTodayUsd`, `riskCheck`, `thesisExpiry`.
- **Production scan shapes:** movers 20/20, actives 30, news 50-cap, IEX marketInfo parity (and the ~40x-floor production finding goes in the report).
- **Leakage machinery, trimmed not dropped:** cutoff verification; probe in two arms with explicit scheduling. Arm 1 (no run dependency): 15 random candidate pairs + 3 positive controls, masked vs unmasked — controls run from T+0:20, candidate pairs during Phase A idle. Arm 2 (traded pairs — inputs exist only after Phase B): every traded (date, ticker) pair, launched at T+3:00 concurrent with the sweep (2 calls/trade; counted in the sweep slot's printed fresh-call budget; spills into the buffer if trade count is large). The leakage bound is stated only if the report asserts both arms completed: positive-control power from arm 1, 100% traded-pair coverage from arm 2.
- **Per-tick kill switch, both halt policies** (halt-policy delta computed per episode — free in Phase B).
- **Exact + canonical LLM caches with token accounting** — the 5h run seeds the cache; a later full-window run reuses every call.

## What is cut, and what each cut costs (the honesty table)

| Cut | Cost to the answer |
|---|---|
| 74 of 124 days never simulated | Wider CIs; rare-regime days may be missed (dispersion stratum mitigates the interesting tail) |
| No compounding equity curve | No max-drawdown-on-$50k path; per-episode drawdown reported instead. Compounding is second-order at expected trade counts |
| Episodes force-flatten at D+1 20:00 | Multi-day orphan dynamics unmeasured — the v1 dangling-position question stays open; flagged, and D+1-20:00 dangling counts are reported as a lower bound |
| Walk-forward dropped | No tuning validation at all — correct at this n; the sweep is explicitly descriptive (abstention/fill-rate vs threshold curves) |
| Sweep grid 18 cells (threshold × bear-weight) not 72 | Loses chase/drop sensitivity; those knobs only matter once entries exist |
| Probe sample smaller | Leakage bound is looser; positive controls still gate whether the probe means anything |

## Five-hour schedule (pipelined; times are budgeted ceilings)

| T+ | Track A (build agents) | Track B (data/run) |
|---|---|---|
| 0:00–0:20 | `data.ts` + fetch script first (T1-lite) | — |
| 0:20–1:30 | Parallel agents: scans, llm-cache, fills, ledger, driver, metrics (T2–T7 compressed, tests included) | **Universe SIP daily bars download + feed probe** (~200–400 paginated requests; budget 40 min), then day ranking + sample selection |
| 1:30–2:15 | Integration: 1 synthetic-episode test green | **Phase A** for 50 episodes (≈500 calls, 16-way ≈ 25–40 min) + leakage probe piggybacked |
| 2:15–3:00 | — | Lazy minute-bar/quote fetch for candidate symbol-days (small); **Phase B** all 50 episodes in parallel + both halt policies |
| 3:00–3:30 | — | 18-cell sweep (Phase B only, canonical judge cache; fresh-call budget incl. probe arm 2 printed first — if it exceeds ~300 calls, threshold floor rises to 0.55) + probe arm 2 (traded pairs) concurrently |
| 3:30–4:15 | — | REPORT.md generation + my read-through with invariant/violation audit |
| 4:15–5:00 | Buffer (data retries, rate limits, one rebuild cycle) | — |

Hard checkpoints: if the bar download hasn't finished by T+1:10, the universe shrinks to symbols ≥ $1M IEX ADV (report notes it). If Phase A hasn't finished by T+2:30, episodes drop to 40 (R=24/H=16) by pre-registered rule, never ad hoc.

## What the 5-hour answer will look like

- **Mechanical:** pass/fail across ~50 episodes × ~130 ticks each — zero gate violations required. This claim is answered as well as the full plan would.
- **Behavioral:** abstention rate with CI from stratum R; entry/veto/rejection/fill-rate anatomy from both strata. Answered adequately.
- **Economic:** directional only — headline is the stratum-R per-episode net P&L bootstrap CI (fees/borrow included) against per-episode T-bill carry + measured LLM cost; H economics reported separately as conditional-on-dispersion. If even H-stratum days produce few or no trades, "does not trade enough at defaults to evaluate economically" IS the solid answer, and the sweep curves show exactly what loosening the threshold would have cost or bought on the same cached verdicts.
- Every validity caveat (sampling rule, episode boundaries, leakage bound, fill optimism) sits in the report next to the numbers it qualifies.

The full rev-2 plan remains the follow-up path: the LLM cache and all fetched data carry over unchanged, so the six-month sequential run later costs only the un-sampled days' calls plus Phase B.
